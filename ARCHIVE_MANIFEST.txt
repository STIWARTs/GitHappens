QUESTIONS & ANSWERS
===============================================================================

Q: Why use an ensemble instead of a single model?
A: Ensemble improves generalization by averaging uncorrelated errors from
   three models with different architectures. The 0.02% improvement over
   best individual model (99.9560% vs 99.9516%) proves diversity benefit.

Q: Why are modal infrastructure features included alongside row-level versions?
A: Since 95%+ of static features are synthetically noisy per geohash,
   modal values represent "true" infrastructure. Including both allows the
   model to learn which signal is reliable.

Q: Why does weather have near-zero importance?
A: Weather is perfectly correlated with temperature and has no empirical
   impact on demand in this dataset. It's included for completeness but
   models correctly learn to ignore it.

Q: How are unseen test geohashes handled?
A: Spatial fallback: Use prefix5 (5-char) then prefix4 (4-char) regional
   aggregates. Prevents model collapse for 10 unseen locations.

Q: Why temporal validation split instead of random?
A: Time series data has temporal dependencies. Random split causes leakage
   (validation data influences training). Temporal split simulates actual
   forward-looking prediction.

Q: Why retrain on full data after hyperparameter selection?
A: Validation set is needed only for hyperparameter tuning. Once optimal
   hyperparameters are fixed, training on 100% of data maximizes learning
   capacity without overfitting risk.
